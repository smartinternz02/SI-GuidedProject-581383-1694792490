Importing Libraries¶

In [1]:
import seaborn as sns
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

Take car crashes dataset from seaborn library¶

Loading It¶

In [2]:
car_crashes = sns.load_dataset("car_crashes")

Perform Data Visualization¶

Plot 1: Pairplot¶

In [3]:
sns.pairplot(car_crashes)
plt.show()

Inference: Pairplot allows us to visualize relationships between numerical variables in the dataset.¶

We can observe scatter plots for each numerical variable combination, and histograms for each individual variable.¶

Plot 2: Heatmap¶

In [4]:
correlation_matrix = car_crashes.corr()
sns.heatmap(correlation_matrix, annot=True, cmap='coolwarm')
plt.title('Correlation Heatmap')
plt.show()

Inference: The heatmap shows the correlation between numerical variables.¶

Variables with higher absolute correlation values are more strongly related.¶

Plot 3: Distribution of Total Crashes¶

In [5]:
sns.histplot(car_crashes['total'], kde=True)
plt.xlabel('Total Crashes')
plt.ylabel('Frequency')
plt.title('Distribution of Total Crashes')
plt.show()

Inference: The histogram provides insight into the distribution of total crashes,¶

showing whether it follows a normal distribution or if there are any outliers.¶

Plot 4: Boxplot¶

In [6]:
sns.boxplot(x='abbrev', y='total', data=car_crashes)
plt.xlabel('State Abbreviation')
plt.ylabel('Total Crashes')
plt.title('Total Crashes by State')
plt.xticks(rotation=45)
plt.show()

Inference: The boxplot allows us to see the distribution of total crashes by state.¶

We can identify potential outliers and compare crash distributions across different states.¶

Plot 5: Violin plot¶

In [7]:
sns.violinplot(x='ins_premium', y='abbrev', data=car_crashes)
plt.xlabel('Insurance Premium')
plt.ylabel('State Abbreviation')
plt.title('Distribution of Insurance Premium by State')
plt.show()

Inference: The violin plot shows the distribution of insurance premiums by state.¶

It provides a visualization of the data distribution and its spread.¶
